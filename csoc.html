Ethical Rules Cross-Reference

This interactive list connects ethical principles from three major IT/technology professional codes: ACM Code of Ethics, Software Engineering Code of Ethics, and TIVIA Ethics Guidelines.

  • 1.5 Respect the work required to produce new ideas
  • 1.6 Respect privacy
  • 1.7 Honor confidentiality
  • 2.1 Strive to achieve high quality in both processes and products
  • 2.2 Maintain high standards of professional competence
  • 2.3 Know and respect existing rules
  • 2.4 Accept and provide appropriate professional review

  • 2.6 Perform work only in areas of competence
  • 2.7 Foster public awareness and understanding
  • 2.8 Access computing resources only when authorized or in public good
  • 2.9 Design and implement systems that are robustly and usably secure
  • 3.1 Public good is central during all professional work
  • 3.2 Articulate, encourage, and evaluate social responsibilities

Software Engineering Code of Ethics and Professional Practice

  • 1.01 Accept full responsibility for their own work
  • 1.02 Moderate the interests of the engineer, employer, client and users with the public good
  • 1.03 Approve software only if safe, meets specs, and does not harm quality of life or privacy
  • 1.04 Disclose actual or potential danger to users or environment
  • 1.05 Cooperate in efforts to address matters of grave public concern
  • 1.06 Be fair and avoid deception in public statements
  • 1.07 Consider physical disabilities, economic disadvantage, and access issues
  • 1.08 Volunteer professional skills to good causes and contribute to public education
  • 2.01 Provide service in their areas of competence
  • 2.02 Not knowingly use software obtained or retained illegally or unethically
  • 2.03 Use client/employer property only with authorization and consent
  • 2.04 Ensure documents are approved when required
  • 2.05 Keep private confidential information where consistent with public interest and law
  • 2.06 Identify, document, and report project risks (failure, cost, legal issues)
  • 2.07 Identify and report significant social concerns in software
  • 2.08 Accept no outside work detrimental to primary employer
  • 2.09 Promote no interest adverse to employer or client unless higher ethical concern is compromised
  • 3.01 Strive for high quality, acceptable cost, and reasonable schedule
  • 3.02 Ensure proper and achievable project goals
  • 3.03 Identify and address ethical, economic, cultural, legal, and environmental issues
  • 3.04 Ensure qualification for any project through education, training, and experience
  • 3.05 Ensure appropriate method is used for any project
  • 3.06 Work to follow professional standards when available
  • 3.07 Strive to fully understand software specifications
  • 3.08 Ensure specifications are well documented, meet user needs, and have approvals
  • 3.09 Ensure realistic cost, schedule, personnel, quality, and outcome estimates
  • 3.10 Ensure adequate testing, debugging, and review
  • 3.11 Ensure adequate documentation, including problems and solutions
  • 3.12 Work to develop software that respects privacy
  • 3.13 Use only accurate data derived by ethical and lawful means
  • 3.14 Maintain data integrity, sensitive to outdated or flawed data
  • 3.15 Treat all software maintenance with same professionalism as new development
  • 4.01 Temper technical judgments with human values
  • 4.02 Only endorse documents prepared under supervision or within competence
  • 4.03 Maintain professional objectivity when evaluating software
  • 4.04 Not engage in deceptive financial practices
  • 4.05 Disclose conflicts of interest that cannot reasonably be avoided
  • 4.06 Refuse to participate in bodies with undisclosed conflicts
  • 5.01 Ensure good management for any project
  • 5.02 Ensure engineers are informed of standards before being held to them
  • 5.03 Ensure engineers know employer policies for protecting passwords and confidential data
  • 5.04 Assign work only after considering education and experience
  • 5.05 Ensure realistic estimates of cost, schedule, personnel, quality, and outcomes
  • 5.06 Attract potential engineers only by full and accurate description of employment
  • 5.07 Offer fair and just remuneration
  • 5.08 Not unjustly prevent someone from taking a suitable position
  • 5.09 Ensure fair agreement on ownership of software, processes, research, etc.
  • 5.10 Provide due process in hearing charges of policy violation
  • 5.11 Not ask engineers to do anything inconsistent with the Code
  • 5.12 Not punish anyone for expressing ethical concerns
  • 6.01 Help develop an organizational environment favorable to ethical behavior
  • 6.02 Promote public knowledge of software engineering
  • 6.03 Extend knowledge by participating in professional organizations
  • 6.04 Support other software engineers striving to follow the Code
  • 6.05 Not promote personal interest at expense of profession, client, or employer
  • 6.06 Obey all laws unless compliance is inconsistent with public interest
  • 6.07 Be accurate in stating characteristics of software, avoiding speculative or misleading claims
  • 6.08 Take responsibility for detecting, correcting, and reporting errors
  • 6.09 Ensure clients and supervisors know of commitment to the Code
  • 6.10 Avoid associations with businesses conflicting with the Code
  • 6.11 Recognize that violations are inconsistent with being a professional software engineer
  • 6.12 Express concerns when significant violations are detected
  • 6.13 Report significant violations to authorities when consultation is impossible or dangerous
  • 7.01 Encourage colleagues to adhere to the Code
  • 7.02 Assist colleagues in professional development
  • 7.03 Credit fully the work of others and refrain from undue credit
  • 7.04 Review work objectively, candidly, and properly documented
  • 7.05 Give a fair hearing to colleagues’ opinions, concerns, or complaints
  • 7.06 Assist colleagues in understanding standard practices (security, confidentiality)
  • 7.07 Not unfairly intervene in a colleague’s career; may question competence for public interest
  • 7.08 In situations outside competence, call upon other professionals
  • 8.01 Further knowledge of software development and management
  • 8.02 Improve ability to create safe, reliable, useful software
  • 8.03 Improve ability to produce accurate, informative documentation
  • 8.04 Improve understanding of software and its environment
  • 8.05 Improve knowledge of relevant standards and laws
  • 8.06 Improve knowledge of the Code, its interpretation, and application
  • 8.07 Not give unfair treatment due to irrelevant prejudices
  • 8.08 Not influence others to act in breach of the Code
  • 8.09 Recognize that personal violations are inconsistent with being a professional

TIVIA Ethics Guidelines

  • Authority and Responsibility IT professionals must not misuse their power; must face up to responsibility
  • Knowledge and Experience Professionals must understand their limits; develop skills; respect confidentiality and client trust
  • Attitude Professionals work not just for themselves, but for others; avoid greed; consider impact on others
  • Communications Professionals must communicate clearly, inform clients of both good and bad news
  • Consequences of Professionals’ Work IT work has chain-of-consequences ending with the user; must understand needs and wants of consumer, client, employer
  • Other People Respect the work of others and their rights to creations; consider human rights, environmental questions, laws, and copyrights
  • Promotion of Ethics Promote ethically sustainable ways of acting; ethics is about character and ongoing awareness of environment and people
